The Oblation is a little over 3 meters tall, representing the 333 years of Spanish colonization of the Philippines.


The Oblation is a visualization of the selfless offering of the Filipino patriots during the Philippine Revolution. It’s based on a stanza in Jose Rizal’s last poem, popularly known as “Mi Ultimo Adios.” He wrote about freedom fighters who were willing to die in the battlefield.

The Oblation

In Quezon City, Philippines, on the campus of the University of the Philippines Diliman, stands a famous bronze statue known as The Oblation. The statue, which was made by national artist Guillermo Tolentino, shows a man facing upward with his arms outstretched and represents selfless devotion and sacrifice for the nation.

About the Artist

Guillermo Tolentino was a renowned Filipino sculptor, celebrated for his significant impact on Philippine art in the 20th century. Born in Malolos, Bulacan, he is best known for creating the iconic Oblation statue at the University of the Philippines Diliman campus. Recognized as a National Artist of the Philippines for Sculpture, Tolentino's works beautifully blend classical aesthetics with Filipino cultural influences. His legacy endures through various monuments and sculptures that grace public spaces, showcasing his profound contribution to the preservation and promotion of the Philippines' artistic heritage.
