The "Blood Compact"

"The Blood Compact" is an 1886 historical painting by Juan Luna, a gift to the Manila city council. It portraysteh 1565 Sandugo, between Datu Sikatuna of Bohol and Miguel Lopez De Legazpi.Sikatuna was described to be 'being crowded out of the picture by Miguel López de Legazpi and his fellow conquistadores'. While the Blood Compact presents an image of equal alliance, it overlooks the violent reality of Spanish conquest and ongoing prejudice against Filipinos.

The Artist

Juan Luna was a late 19th-century Filipino painter, sculptor, and political activist who participated in the Philippine Revolution. He increased to popularity as one of the first known Filipino artists.
